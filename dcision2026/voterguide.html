Ward 1
In 2025, the DC Council scaled back Tenant Opportunity to Purchase (TOPA) rights, including ending TOPA for buildings with 2–4 units. Do you support these changes, and if not, what TOPA rights would you work to restore?
Restore all TOPA rights
Brown calls the 2–4 unit changes “shameful” and wants to reinstate those rights, reverse single-family and downtown exemptions, and strengthen TOPA funding. Deramo would reverse the 2–4 unit exemption and shorten the 15-year new construction window. Lynch opposes any watering down and would close loopholes letting housing trusts capture affordable stock. Raj strongly supports TOPA as an essential displacement-prevention tool.
Rashida Brown
ANC Commissioner
Miguel Trindade Deramo

Accepts the 2–4 unit exemption as a reasonable balance
Reyes understands that owners of 2–4 unit buildings are often long-term family homeowners — not investors. She believes the Council struck a reasonable balance that supports responsible ownership while protecting tenants in larger buildings.
Jackie Reyes
Candidate
Ward 1
Do you support increasing taxes on households with high incomes or tremendous wealth, and on highly profitable corporations? If so, list the tax policy changes you would support.
Yes — with specific proposals
Brown: business activity tax (BAT) at 1.5–2%, tiered capital gains (1% on income $500K–$750K, 2% on $750K–$1M, 3% over $1M), mansion tax on luxury property sales over $3M, progressive estate tax. Deramo: tiered/mansion property tax, split-rate property tax (taxes land value to encourage productive use), BAT, lower estate tax exemption and decouple from federal stepped-up basis. Raj: raise capital gains rates and close the stepped-up basis loophole, wealth tax on households with over $30M in assets, mark-to-market taxation, lower estate tax exemption, BAT at 2% (projected to raise ~$500M/year by taxing large law firms, lobbying shops, and consulting giants currently exempt from DC taxes).
